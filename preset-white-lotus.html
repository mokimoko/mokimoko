SillyTavern Preset

White Lotus

A preset built to get LLMs out of their own way — more initiative, more creativity, fewer stalled scenes.

Tested on GLM, Kimi, Deepseek, and Gemma. Best suited for character-driven and plot-focused roleplay.

About

A longstanding annoyance with AI roleplay is models refusing to take initiative or be creative. They write beautiful prose, set up vivid scenes, even offer some internal commentary — but when it comes to actually introducing unexpected elements or pushing the scene forward without the user guiding? It's a struggle.

White Lotus aims to fix that. It's less "instruction-heavy" than most presets, which is intentional — the tradeoff between token efficiency and more flawed, human-sounding output is worth it. It includes a Variety Toggle that randomizes additional tags to introduce unexpected elements, and optional CoT prompting for thinking models that want to really chew on the scene before responding.

This preset is built for roleplay that cares about plot and characters. It's probably terrible for RPG-mechanic-style roleplay — fair warning.

How To Use
  • 01 Set your variables — tense, POV, and cultural setting. These drive the style outputs.
  • 02 Fill in CORE with your character card, persona info, and scenario as usual.
  • 03 Pick your optional modules from MODULES. Anti-Slop, Anti-Positivity, and Anti-Roboticism are good starting points.
  • 04 Toggle Variety Toggle when you want more dynamic, unpredictable responses. It adds a randomized element to the next generation — great for breaking out of repetitive loops.
  • 05 Untamed CoT (Chain of Thought) works best with thinking models. It prompts the AI to work through the scene before responding, which noticeably improves initiative and creativity.
